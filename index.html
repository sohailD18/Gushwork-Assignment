Frequently Asked Questions

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) pipe is a flexible, durable plastic piping system used for water supply, gas distribution, sewer systems, and industrial applications. It's known for its corrosion resistance and long service life.

HDPE pipes offer superior corrosion resistance, leak-proof fusion joints, flexibility for easy installation, chemical resistance, and a service life of 50+ years. They're lighter than metal pipes and require minimal maintenance.

HDPE pipes are joined using heat fusion methods including butt fusion, electrofusion, and extrusion welding. These create monolithic, leak-proof joints that are as strong as the pipe itself.

HDPE pipes are available in various pressure ratings including PN 2.5, PN 4, PN 6, PN 8, PN 10, PN 12.5, and PN 16. The rating indicates the pressure in bar at 20°C.

HDPE pipes have a design life of 50-100 years depending on operating conditions, pressure rating, and installation quality. They're backed by ISO 4427 and IS 5984 standards.
